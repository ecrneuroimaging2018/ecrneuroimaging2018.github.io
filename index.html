ECRs in Neuroimaging Day Abstract Submission FAQ

How do I submit an abstract?

Please send titles and abstracts (no more than 300 words for talks and no more than 200 words for power pitches) to l.quadt@bsms.ac.uk or k.themelis@bsms.ac.uk. Please state whether you are a postdoc or PhD student so we can spread talks evenly across slots.

What is the difference between a talk and a power pitch?

A talk will be a 10-15 minutes presentation with an additional five minutes for questions or discussion. A power pitch will be a 3-5 minutes presentation during which you can pitch your research to the audience. You can use max of 3 slides or just 1 slide stating your name and affiliation.

What should my presentation be about?

As we are a community of Early Career Researchers, we welcome with equal merit i) talks presenting recent results, ii) talks describing new techniques you are developing whilst in post and iii) talks outlining your on-going work with preliminary results. You can also choose to talk about a method that you think more people should know about, or the impact of your research.
In every case, we strongly encourage speakers to spend time putting their work and potential impact into a wider context for the audience, making the research accessible to those outside your immediate field. We want everyone to get as much out of the day as possible and gain a broader understanding of the work our community is doing. We hope you can take discussions into the very finest details during breaks and the evening!

Selection

We will be choosing talks to cover a broad spectrum of research, and will preferentially select abstracts (and therefore talks) that are pitched towards the broader neuroimaging community.

As a guide, abstracts should:

  • cover material using neuroimaging techniques (MRI, M/EEG, TMS/tDCS), or be related to the interpretation of neuroimaging results within humans
  • cover current research at Sussex (or for ECRs who have recently joined, recent research conducted elsewhere)
  • include definitions of specialist terms/acronyms
  • give a brief context of the research in the wider field

The abstract submission deadline is Monday, 7th May 2018. We will be in further contact with notification of who has been allocated a talk in the middle of May. Thanks for contributing!
